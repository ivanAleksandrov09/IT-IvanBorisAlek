Water transportation: Submarine

Submarines are watercraft that are capable of operating underwater.
They are used for a variety of purposes, including military, scientific, and commercial applications.
Submarines are equipped with a variety of systems and technologies that allow them to navigate and operate underwater,
including ballast tanks, propulsion systems, and sonar systems.
Submarines are typically powered by nuclear reactors or diesel engines, and can travel at high speeds and depths.
They are used for a variety of missions, including surveillance, reconnaissance, and combat operations.

Sonars

Sonar is a technique that uses sound propagation to navigate,
communicate with or detect objects on or under the surface of the water.
Two types of technology share the name "sonar":
passive sonar is essentially listening for the sound made by vessels;
active sonar is emitting pulses of sounds and listening for echoes.
Submarines rely on sonar to a greater extent than surface ships as they cannot use radar in water
